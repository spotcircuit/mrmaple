Abies alba 'Green Spiral'
Weeping Silver Fir

Zones 5-8

﻿'Green Spiral' is an unusual fir selection. Unlike many other conifers, the central leader on this selection doesn't grow in a uniform upright fashion. The central leader often twists and curves, creating a spiral like growth habit. This is extremely attractive, but also makes each specimen uniquely different. As our friend Mark Dwyer states, "No two specimens are alike" with 'Green Spiral'. 'Green Spiral' may reach up to 8 ft in height in 10 years. Our 'Green Spiral' are likely grafted onto Abies koreana.

Abies alba 'Schwarzwald'
Silver Fir

Zones 5 - 7

 

'Schwarzwald' is a unique small growing fir selection. It was found as a witches' broom by Günter Eschrich near the town of Badenweiler, Germany. 'Schwarzwald' has a tighter habit reaching 1 ft in height by 2 ft in width in 10 years. Our Abies alba 'Schwarzwald' are grafted onto Abies koreana. Being small and compact, 'Schwarzwald' makes a great addition to any small garden or conifer trough.

Abies cilicica
Taurus Fir

Zones 

Abies cilicica is known as the Taurus Fir and can be found naturally in Turkey at elevations from 2,500 ft to 7,000 ft. Abies cilicica has a nice pyramidal form reaching 8 ft in height by 4 ft in width in 10 years.  The needles are green but have a silver cast on the back side of the leaves creating an overall silver glow the tree. As Abies cilicica ages it develops a nice bark feature similar to pines that adds an extra interest to this evergreen. Our Abies cilicica are grafted on to Abies koreana.

Abies koreana 'Vengels
Dwarf Korean Fir

Zones 5-7

 

The origin of 'Vengels' upright Korean fir tree remains unknown, yet its distinctiveness merits a place in any conifer garden. Its seed cones are a fascinating horticultural anomaly, displaying a unique purple-blue hue and bristly texture that persists, often sprouting new shoots from their tops. Whether they produce viable seeds is still uncertain.

After 10 years, a mature specimen typically stands at 4 to 5 ft tall and spans 2 ft  wide.

Abies lasiocarpa 'Glacier Blue'

Dwarf Alpine Fir

Zones 4-8

Abies lasiocarpa 'Glacier Blue', also known as the Dwarf Alpine Fir, is a stunning conifer with vibrant blue-silver needles that retain their striking color all year. 'Glacier Blue' is highly valued for its bold color and compact size, making it a standout in any landscape or conifer collection.
